BEIJING WU

I am a Principal Scientist in the Reagent and Assay Development at Roche Diagnostic Solutions based in Pleasanton, California. I lead a team of assay development scientists to create a suite of NextGen Sequencing assays for scientists and clinical researchers to conduct genomic studies for better understanding the genetic causes of health and disease.

I am a graduate student at the University of California, Berkeley, studying for a Master of Information and Data Science (expected graduation Summer 2023).

Previously, I worked as a scientific researcher in Dr. William Greenleaf's group at Stanford University, School of Medicine. My project focused on the development of a novel epigenomic method: the assay of transposase-accessible chromatin with high-throughput sequencing in single cells (scATAC-seq), which led me to 10 peer reviewed publications in prestigious journals such as Nature, Nature Genetics and Cell.

I have over 10 years of experience in the biomedical field. My career goal is to maximize the synergy between genomic medicine and biomedical informatics to provide patients with precisely targeted treatment recommendations. By developing the tools and quantitative methods needed to discern biological signals and elucidate patterns in large data sets, I hope to inform product strategies and guide assay and bioinformatic pipeline development.
